Psychology

The Anatomy of a
Liminal Space

DEFINITION
"Transitional areas—places you pass through, not places you stay. When these spaces are empty, the brain struggles to process the lack of context."
  • Transitional thresholds (hallways, waiting rooms)
  • Kenophobia: the fear of empty voids
  • Absence of intended purpose creates tension
Before we delve into the lore, we must understand the psychology. Liminal spaces are transitional areas—places you pass through, not places you stay. When these spaces are empty, our brains struggle to process the lack of context. This triggers kenophobia, the fear of empty voids, making the mundane feel deeply hostile.

The phenomenon began on May 12, 2019, on a 4chan paranormal thread. An anonymous user posted a picture of an empty, yellow office space. Another user replied with the foundational lore: if you noclip out of reality, you end up here. The mono-yellow wallpaper, the damp carpet, and the hum of fluorescent lights were born in this single post.

Entities

Denizens of the Void

THREAT: HIGH

Smilers

Hostile entities that lurk in pure darkness. They are drawn to light sources and characterized by their glowing, wide-toothed grins. Do not break eye contact.

THREAT: VARIES

Facelings

Humanoid figures entirely lacking facial features. Often behave like normal humans but trigger severe uncanny valley responses. Behavior ranges from docile to violently hostile.

THREAT: EXTREME

Hounds

Aggressive, biomechanical anomalies walking on all fours. Highly territorial and fast. Paranoia of being hunted escalates when their growls echo down the corridors.
