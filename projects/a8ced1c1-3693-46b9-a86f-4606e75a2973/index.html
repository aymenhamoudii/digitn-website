Health isn't just for emergencies • Small habits = big benefits • Campus resources are easier than you think

Welcome students with energy. Acknowledge that health care sounds like a boring adult thing, but frame it as essential for crushing their academic and social goals. Mention that this won't be a lecture.

THE REALITY CHECK

You're Not Invincible (Sorry)

80% skip annual checkups

Young adults skip doctor visits because they "feel fine"—but feeling fine isn't the same as being healthy. That cough you ignored for three weeks? It could've been caught early.

Today's skip = tomorrow's emergency

Preventable issues become expensive emergencies. Catching things early is cheaper, easier, and way less stressful than dealing with a crisis during finals week.

Mental health = GPA

Stress, anxiety, and untreated mental health issues directly impact your grades and graduation rates. Your mind is an organ too—take care of it.

Break the invincibility myth gently. Use relatable examples like 'that cough you ignored for three weeks' or stress affecting finals. Emphasize that catching things early is cheaper and easier.

MYTHBUSTING

Let's Clear the Air

"I'm too young for serious issues"

Reality: Diabetes, hypertension, and mental health conditions often start in your 20s. Early detection changes everything.

BUSTED

"I can't afford insurance"

Reality: Most students stay on parents' plans until 26. Campus health centers often provide free or low-cost care.

BUSTED

"The ER is fine for routine stuff"

Reality: ERs are for emergencies. You'll wait hours and pay 24x more than student health for basic care.

BUSTED

"Mental health struggles make me weak"

Reality: 1 in 4 college students seek mental health support. Asking for help is strength, not weakness.

BUSTED
Address common student misconceptions with empathy, not judgment. For the insurance myth, mention most students are covered by parents or school plans. Keep the tone casual—'We've all thought this'.
